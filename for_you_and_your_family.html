Why Organic – For You & Your Family

Better food choices for healthier lives, today and tomorrow.

Today almost everyone is suffering from health problems related to stomach, indigestion, loss of energy, overall and general tiredness. When you compare the health and appetite of our ancestors and our previous generations, the difference is obvious. Almost all mothers complain that their children are not eating well enough. We feel heavy even if we’ve consumed small portions of food.

Almost everyday new scientific evidence is being produced that link many dreaded diseases such as diabetes, heart strokes, cancers, etc. to the food presently being produced and consumed. A large number of young professionals today gain weight even if they are eating very little. Surely the food we eat must have something to do with these issues.

What Organic Food Really Means

Organic food is a sincere attempt to improve the quality of life through the food we eat. While it is contamination-free in terms of toxic chemicals, it is also handled in a manner that makes it absolutely adulteration-free.

The certification process across the value chain requires that at each stage of cultivation, procurement, storage and processing is verified through third-party independent inspection and certification agencies. Organic food is sold in packed and sealed form with certification logos, making it one of the most closely monitored foods available today.

  • ✔ Third-party certified
  • ✔ Sealed & traceable
  • ✔ No adulteration
  • ✔ Highest consumer credibility

Organic food is free from the potent cocktail of fertilizers, pesticides, insecticides and other chemicals that go into growing conventional produce. Chemical preservation methods are not allowed during storage, making organic food safer and healthier.

There are many reasons for the worldwide acceptance of organic foods. Many find that organic food tastes better, as farmers use traditional seed varieties grown in naturally suitable areas, delivering authentic flavours rather than synthetic tastes.

Quality-conscious consumers willingly choose organic foods even if they are slightly more expensive, knowing that the best often comes at an extra cost.

In the last 20 years globally, and in the last decade in India, there has been a rapid rise in demand for certified organic food. Market surveys, consumer research and focused interviews consistently confirm this trend.

Negative Preference Route

Consumers concerned about contamination, adulteration and the harmful effects of food on human health.

Positive Preference Route

Environmentally conscious consumers concerned about sustainability, the Earth and future generations.

What Research Says About Organic Food

Organic food reduces exposure to carcinogenic pesticides, helping preserve children's health and that of future generations.

Organic foods contain no toxins, artificial preservatives, antibiotics or growth promoters.

Organic foods have significantly lower chemical and nitrate levels compared to conventional produce.

Organic foods are richer in vitamins and minerals and offer greater diversity and authentic flavours.

While the scientific community debates nutritional differences between conventional and organic food, consumers increasingly choose organic based on the belief that it will not cause harm. This belief, grounded in safety and sustainability, continues to guide informed food choices.

Organic Philosophy

For the Earth & Future

Organic philosophy is inherently beneficial to the Earth, environment and thus our future. Organic production and processing is based on a number of principles and ideas generally defined as Standards. These cover organic production at the farm, processing, storage, labelling and marketing activities â€” the complete value chain from farming to food.

Organic agriculture also aims to minimize the use of external inputs to reduce the cost of cultivation, thus benefiting both the producer as well as the consumers.

Organic agriculture covers all kinds of food and fibre production systems and can be defined as a chemical-free farming system that produces uncontaminated farm produce of high nutritional quality in sufficient quantities. It allows producers to obtain adequate returns, satisfaction from their work and a safe working environment, while creating an ecologically, socially and economically sustainable system of food and fibre production.

Principal Aims & Objectives of Organic Production and Processing

  • To produce sufficient quantities of high quality food, fibre and other products.
  • To work compatibly with natural cycles and living systems through soil, plants and animals.
  • To recognize the wider social and ecological impact within organic production systems.
  • To maintain and increase long-term soil fertility using biological and mechanical methods.
  • To maintain and encourage agricultural and natural biodiversity on farms and surroundings.
  • To conserve genetic diversity through on-farm management of genetic resources.
  • To use renewable resources and avoid pollution and waste.
  • To foster local and regional production and distribution.
  • To create a harmonious balance between crop production and animal husbandry.
  • To provide living conditions allowing animals to express natural behaviour.
  • To utilize biodegradable, recyclable and recycled packaging materials.
  • To ensure quality of life, safety and health for everyone involved.
  • To support the establishment of an entire production, processing and distribution chain that is socially just and ecologically responsible.
  • To recognize, protect and learn from indigenous knowledge and traditional farming systems.

With these objectives, it is no wonder that the world today recognizes organic agriculture as a truly sustainable farming system.
